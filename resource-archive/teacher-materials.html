Teacher's Instructions

Featuring a concise teacher's manual and an emotional patient story.

Teacher's Manual

New material is often very hard to digest, and existing educational kits do not do anything to help that. Their teacher manuals are often dense and longwinded, making it hard for educators to learn new lessons. We tried to fix that problem by writing our instructions with readability in mind. Teachers are given enough background knowledge on the brian and Alzheimer's to answer any questions that may occur in the classroom without having to get a degree in neuroscience. Activities are explained in a detailed, step by step fashion with graphical illustrations to help understanding.

Patient Story

Our kit includes a story that follows the life of Albert Z. Heimer. Al lives a fufilling life, full of laughter and joy with his granddaughter Helen, who visits every day after school to play cards and have fun. One day, however, Al starts to experience some early symptoms of Alzheimer's Disease. He begins misplacing his belongings, losing his sense of direction, and forgetting the things and people he loves. Al attempts to cope with his symptoms and keep his condition secret, but it eventually gets so bad that he can no longer keep it a secret. He learns how to cope, all while teaching the readers of his story about what it feels like to have Alzheimer's and what it can be like to love someone with the disease.

Teacher's Manual

Here is the teacher's manual. The teacher's manuals include all the background information necessary for teachers to understand in order to be able to effectively teach and field questions about the brain, neurons, Alzheimer's disease, our Eli Lilly molecule, and clinical trials. In the 14 pages we also include the suggested lesson plans for each of the parts of our kit.

Patient Story

Included in the kit is also an emotional story about a man named Albert Zachary Heimer (Al Z. Heimer). This story documents his experience with developing and dealing with Alzheimer's disease. As a great way to incorporate reading into science, Al's story is the central element of our project.
