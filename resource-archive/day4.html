Day 4 - The Changing Brain

Students will experience the frustration of what it is like living with Alzheimer's Disease

Learning to forget

In the story, Al's condition takes a turn for the worse. His Alzheimer's symptoms get more severe, so on this day the students experience a simulated version of Alzheimer's disease.

Wacky Wordies Worksheet 2

To simulate how Alzheimer's disease ravages the ability to think critically, the students will repeat the critical thinking puzzles from the first day but with extra challenges. These puzzles here are much harder, and some of them don't even have solutions! Along with solving the critical thinking puzzles, the students will also play a harder version of the memory card game and participate in a mini mental status exam. Often times when a person has Alzheimer's disease, the people around them feel intense frustration at their loved ones' inability to remember things. This will help frustrated people to better understand the people they care about.

Follow-Up Worksheet 4

This lesson will likely be very frustrating for the kids, but they will still likely need to review what they did that day. This worksheet includes drawing comparisons, drawing picutres, writing about opinions, and thinking about their futures.
